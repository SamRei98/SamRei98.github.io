Data Science Portfolio

Samuli Reinikainen

Hello! You are at my webpage. Here is a little to know about me.

I am a data science post-grad student from Tampere University. I did my bachelor's from mathematics and statistics and was especially interested in computational statistics. From there it was easy to gravitate towards machine learning and AI which have later become my main interests. I have always been a passionate problem solver and I really enjoy solving real-life problems with my data-driven toolbox. On a high level, my main skills include computational statistics, machine learning and data visualization. In terms of technology that means R, Python, Java, C++, SQL, Tensorflow, Spark and the list goes on. Currently I am looking to expand my knowledge in deep learning techniques, especially for natural language and image processing.

I have a strong professional background in applied machine learning. The specific problems I have been working on at MaxiPoint OY include predictive maintenance - that is prediction of asset failure from sensor data - and cost prediction of various industrial processes. Professionally, I have also been involved in java development, database management and software testing. On a hobbyist level, I am really interested in sports data modeling, especially in football.There is something about predicting the outcome of such an inherently random series of events as a football match that I find really intriguing. These insights can also be applied to the sports betting market which I also have a growing interest in.

Outside of work I really enjoy football. Watching it, analyzing it and even trying to dodge balls as a low level goalkeeper. I also like running, gaming, fishing and spending time in nature.

See my projects for some examples of things I have been working on.

Academic

Bachelor's thesis

The thesis for my BSc degree in mathematics and statistics. The topic is principal component analysis and parametric survival models for remaining useful lifetime predictions for turbofan engines using the famous Turbofan Engine Degradation Simulation dataset. The thesis was graded 5/5 and is in Finnish.

Skills: statistics, academic writing
